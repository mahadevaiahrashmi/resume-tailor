# Easy Guide — Resume Tailor

A friendly, step-by-step guide. No technical background needed. It helps you turn
a job posting + your current resume into a polished **one-page resume and cover
letter** you can download as Word and PDF.

---

## What this tool does

You give it two things:
1. The **job posting** you want to apply for.
2. Your **current resume** (as text).

It gives you back **four files** — your resume and a matching cover letter, each
in Word and PDF — rewritten to fit that specific job. It only rearranges and
re-words your *real* experience. It will **not** make up jobs or skills you don't
have.

## Before you start

Someone may have already set this up for you. If so, just open your web browser
and go to the address you were given (usually **http://127.0.0.1:8000**). You'll
see a page titled **Resume Tailor**.

If a box on the page says an engine is **"not detected"**, that's okay — pick one
that doesn't say that. The **Mock** option always works and is great for seeing
how your documents will look.

## Step by step

### 1. Choose an "AI engine"
At the top there's a dropdown called **AI engine**. Think of this as *which
helper writes your documents*. The page picks an available one for you, so you
can usually leave it as-is. You can ignore the small "model" box next to it.

> **What's an engine?** It's the program that does the rewriting. Some run on
> your own computer (private); one option ("Mock") just reshuffles your text so
> you can preview the layout.

### 2. Paste the job posting
In the box labelled **Job description**, paste the whole job advert — copy it from
the company's careers page and paste it in.

### 3. Paste your resume
In the box labelled **Your current resume**, paste your existing resume as plain
text. Don't worry about formatting; the tool handles that.

### 4. (Optional) Add a note
In **Extra instructions**, you can type a wish in plain English, like:
*"Focus on my teamwork and leadership."* You can also leave this empty.

### 5. Click "Generate documents"
Press the big blue **Generate documents** button. A colored bar tells you what's
happening:
- **Amber** = working (please wait — the first time can take up to a minute).
- **Green** = done!
- **Red** = something went wrong (the message tells you what).

### 6. Download your files
When it's done, you'll see four **Download** buttons:
- Resume — PDF
- Resume — Word
- Cover Letter — PDF
- Cover Letter — Word

Click each to save it. You'll also see a **Preview** on the page so you can read
them first. Use the **Resume** and **Cover letter** tabs to switch between them.

## Tips

- **Use PDF to apply, Word to edit.** PDFs look the same everywhere; open the
  Word file if you want to tweak wording.
- **Keep it true.** If you see anything that isn't accurate, edit the Word file —
  your resume should always be honest.
- **Try again freely.** Change your instructions and click Generate again to get
  a different emphasis.

## Common questions

**Is my information private?**
Yes. Your text goes only to the helper running on this computer. It is not
uploaded to this tool's makers.

**It says an engine is "not detected." Did I break it?**
No. That engine just isn't installed on this computer. Choose another from the
dropdown — **Mock** always works.

**The button is spinning for a while.**
That's normal the first time, especially with a private on-computer engine. Give
it up to a minute.

**Can I change the wording afterwards?**
Absolutely — open the **Word** version and edit it like any document.
